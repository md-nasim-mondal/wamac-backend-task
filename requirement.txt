Background Story
Karim, the lead backend engineer of NovaPay, had spent months preparing for the
year-end payroll season. Two hundred corporate clients were scheduled to disburse
salaries simultaneously. Servers were scaled. The team was confident.​
Then the first call came in.​
A corporate HR manager reported that 340 employees had received their salaries twice.
Karim checked the logs. There were none. The client's system had retried the API after a
timeout, and NovaPay had no idempotency protection. Every retry created a new
disbursement. The employer's account went $180,000 negative. Overdraft protection
never triggered because the balance check and the debit ran in two separate queries with
no lock between them.
Before Karim could respond, a second crisis surfaced. Employees were seeing money
credited to their accounts, but transfers were failing. The transaction service had written
the debit, but crashed before writing the credit. The ledger was unbalanced. Money had
vanished inside the system with no recovery path.​
Then the FX team raised an alarm. International transfers initiated at 9:00 am were being
settled at 9:00 am rates. It was now 10:45 am. Rates had moved. Customers were debited
at stale prices without warning. One customer lost $320 on a $2,000 transfer.​
And then the final blow. The transaction history endpoint ran a live query across 40 million
rows on every page load. Under the surge, the database hit 100% CPU and stopped
responding. Six hundred thousand users opened the app to find empty balance screens.
Panic spread on social media within minutes.​
By 3 am, NovaPay had processed $180,000 in duplicate disbursements, lost $94,000 in
incomplete transfers, mis-settled 28 international transactions, and crashed the database
for 22 minutes.
Karim resigned. The board hired a new team. That team is you.​
